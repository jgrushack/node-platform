# 1. Identity, Values & Governance

## Our Values & Culture

*Who we are and what we stand for: NODE's mission, our value pillars, and how we live the Burning Man principles. Start here if you're new.*

### Our Mission

NODE builds aspirational playgrounds for future builders (you) in a forward-thinking (crazy awesome) way with a futuristic voice, helping you feel excited about the future and how bright it is.

### Our Value Pillars

Almost everything we make and do at NODE grows from six pillars. Each one carries a few sub-values we care about:

- **🎶 Music:** curation, performance, cohesion
- **🤸 Movement:** dancing, yoga, exercise
- **🫂 Community:** inclusivity, communication, collaboration
- **🦚 Expression:** creativity, art, fashion
- **🎭 Self:** nutrition, hydration, reflection
- **🧐 Curiosity:** imagination, initiative, humility

### Living the 10 Principles

The 10 Principles of Burning Man aren't just an idea but a guiding set of rules that help make Black Rock City possible. We come back to them all year: during every monthly camp call we discuss the principles and what they mean to us, and the NODE application displays the 10 principles and asks you to pick your favorite. Throughout the planning season, we keep hitting on different principles in our emails so everyone understands how important they are to Burning Man.

Two principles matter especially at NODE:

**Radical Inclusion.** Everyone deserves to feel welcome and included, regardless of background or beliefs. By creating an environment that values inclusivity, we build a strong sense of community and connection among our campers.

**Radical Self-Reliance.** NODE gives you a lot of support and resources, but we also encourage you to be self-sufficient and take responsibility for your own needs. Everyone should be prepared to fix their own broken bike, cook their own meals, and contribute to the community in meaningful ways. By promoting self-reliance, we empower you to take ownership of your Burning Man experience and develop important life skills along the way.

Two more principles take real effort:

**Participation.** Not everyone feels confident about how to get involved and make a meaningful contribution, and that's okay. Camp leads are here to provide clear guidance and encouragement so you can participate actively. If you're not sure how to plug in, just ask.

**Civic Responsibility.** We work to keep NODE a safe, respectful space, both at the festival and within camp. That means being mindful of each other's needs, leaving no trace, and following the event's rules so everyone feels comfortable and included.

### The 11th Principle: Consent

Beyond the official ten, NODE holds Consent as the 11th principle. Everyone is told there is a **zero-strike policy** at NODE: if anyone does not embody consent, they will be asked to leave immediately. There are no warnings. See the Code of Conduct for how consent and sexual safety are handled.

## The Node Foundation Charter

*The mission and purpose of The Node Foundation, our 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the legal and charitable backbone behind the camp.*

### Mission

To work towards a brighter future through community collaboration, art, music, and experience.

### Purpose

The Node Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the sum of its community members working towards a network of art, music, and experience, guiding discovery through events and charitable work. Our goal is to foster a supportive and inclusive environment that encourages creativity, collaboration, and personal growth, for our community and the world at large.

### Areas of Focus

1. **Community Building:** Organize and maintain a communal camp at Burning Man, providing infrastructure and resources to support the well-being and engagement of members. Facilitate regular meetings, workshops, and social events to build a sense of community and belonging among members and the greater public.
2. **Art and Creativity:** Provide resources, funding, and space for artists to develop and showcase their work, fostering a culture of creativity and innovation.
3. **Education and Skill Sharing:** Promote the exchange of knowledge and skills among members and the broader community.
4. **Sustainability and Environmental Responsibility:** Implement and promote sustainable practices such as waste reduction, recycling, and the use of renewable resources, leaving our world in a better place than we found it. Educate members on environmental stewardship and encourage eco-friendly practices both at events and in daily life.
5. **Inclusion and Diversity:** Create an inclusive and welcoming environment for individuals of all backgrounds, ensuring diversity is celebrated and respected while actively working to remove barriers to participation and foster a culture of openness and acceptance.
6. **Support and Outreach:** Engage in outreach activities to support and collaborate with local communities and nonprofit organizations.

## Code of Conduct

*The cornerstone agreement for every NODE member: how we treat each other, the camp, and the playa. Read it before you head to the desert.*

### 🚩 Vibe Check

Burning Man is mayhem. Although this code of conduct lays out a lot of the common-sense, golden-rule guidelines, there is no way it can encapsulate all of the unexpected edge cases, interpersonal conflict, or unquantifiable incidents. NODE leadership reserves the right to make judgement calls on whether an individual is crossing the line, clashing with the ethos of our camp, or disrupting the sanctuary of our desert home. For lack of a better term: a vibe check. If someone is demonstrating a severe lack of effort, inflicting their default-world emotional baggage on others, or attending BM for selfish reasons, a different camp may be a better option.

**TL;DR:** If your particular brand of humanity is hindering the smooth operation of our camp, you may be asked to step away from NODE to protect the positive experience of other campers.

### 🧘 Personal Space

At NODE, intimacy begins with respect. Always be mindful of others' physical and emotional space. Hugs are magic, but not mandatory. Read the room and the body language. No barging into other people's tents unannounced, or popping into a random RV to use the bathroom because all the stalls were taken.

### 🍆 Sexual Safety

Consent isn't just sexy, it's sacred. We uphold a strict **zero-strike policy**: any violation of consent will result in immediate removal from camp. Promiscuity and celebrating your body are part of what make Burning Man magical and empowering. Do not cross the line and take advantage of our communal trust. Nipple pasties? Let em' breathe ✅. Shirt-cocking ❌? Save it for the orgy dome. Common-sense stuff.

### 💰 Payment of Dues

Civic responsibility includes financial contribution. Your dues keep the cold brew flowing, the beats dropping, and the infrastructure standing. It's the bare minimum you can do to contribute to NODE, and issues with payment show an ignorance of the immense amount of capital it takes to pull off the event. If you're unsure about your dues, talk to a camp lead. Transparency is our jam. "Forgetting" to pay dues is a one-way ticket to open camping next year.

### 🫂 Inclusivity

Burning Man is where strangers become soulmates. Yes, plenty of us know each other from the default world. That doesn't mean those are the only people you ride out to the trash fence with. If you see someone on the fringe, pull them into the dance circle. We rise by lifting others.

### 🐴 Illicit Substances

Respect yourself, your community, and the law. NODE is not a place for reckless behavior, and we do not condone or approve of any illegal substances. Burning Man is on federal land, which makes marijuana illegal. Absolutely no smoking weed within view or smell of the public. Drug use should be treated with the utmost sensitivity. Make sure you know exactly what you're putting in your body. Do not discuss usage loudly, as that can beg for a visit from the police or feds.

If you're altering your consciousness, stay grounded in awareness and safety. No peer pressure, no shame, only care and consent. The Zendo is a great on-playa resource if you're not having a good time. Keep an eye on your friends, and speak up if you feel like something is off. NODE keeps narcan and fentanyl testing strips in our first aid kit.

### 🫡 Representing NODE On-Playa

Out in the wild of Black Rock City, you are NODE. Be kind, curious, and creative. Share your water, your wisdom, and your weirdness. We are the camp that gives more than we take.

### ☎️ Camp Calls

Show up. Engage. These aren't just logistics: they're how we build our dream before we build our city. Your voice matters. Calls are also how we get to know new members before heading into the desert.

### ❌ Strike

What goes up, must come down. Strike is a team effort and the camp's #1 priority starting Saturday morning. NODE strikes in the morning and afternoon before heading to the Man Burn and Temple. Abandoning strike duties to go explore the playa only puts a greater workload on the rest of the camp. When the whole camp works together to pack up, it gets done faster and allows for more time to enjoy BRC during the last few days of the event. Work hard, then play harder after.

### ⛺ Camp Life

#### Camp Resources & Amenities
It's your responsibility to familiarize yourself with the camp layout, storage containers, and how our camp operates. Knowing what's where prevents chaos and keeps our camp humming. Read the Notion or the NODE bible to brief yourself, and ask questions instead of for favors.

#### Camp Guests
Bring the vibes, but also bring the introductions. If your friends visit NODE, introduce them to the crew. Our hospitality runs deep, but trust is built with names and handshakes.

#### Social Media
Snap responsibly. Capture joy, not people's privacy. If you post, ask. Respect the magic and mystery of the playa. Some moments are meant to stay dusty. NODEs are here to be in the moment, not to sit in the public tent on Instagram. Refrain from posting publicly during Burn week.

#### Striking Your Tent, A/C, and Bike
Just because your burn ends doesn't mean your stuff vanishes. You are responsible for having your tent disassembled and out from under the large green shade structure at the time designated by camp leads. Bikes and A/Cs should be stored in NR1 when announced by camp leads, and failure to do so impedes our ability to strike the rest of camp. Strike with the same love and effort you used to set up.

### 🙏 Respect of Camp

#### Shower Length
Think spa vibes, not spa hours. Water is gold. Be mindful, be quick, and maybe bring a playlist under five minutes.

#### Tent Electricity
Don't treat the power strip like it's Coachella VIP. Share, rotate, and turn off when not in use. Use A/Cs to cool off, not to keep your tent an icebox 24/7.

#### Kitchen
Clean your dishes, close and secure the fridges, and stay out of the kitchen when people are cooking. Don't hoard ice. Keep the snack area clean. And remember: second helpings are cool, but only after everyone's had firsts.

#### Bikes
Park smart, don't block paths, use a kickstand, and use chargers with love and logic. Only enter and exit the camp through the designated areas. Being negligent with your bike can be dangerous to other campers, obstruct service vehicles, or damage your bike. When riding around BRC, please respect the strict 5 mph speed limit. Zipping across the playa puts a target on your back for rangers. It's about the journey, not the destination.

#### Bathrooms
Treat our portable porcelain thrones with reverence and respect. Wipe spills, tidy up, and keep your belongings out of communal spaces. Yes, they're co-ed. That means being considerate, civil, and conscious of others' privacy. Limit hand washing. It just wastes water, dries your skin out, and dust is the best soap anyways. Lubricate toilet bowls with water before doing your business. If you see an issue like a clogged toilet, leaky pipe, or overflowing tank, please tell one of the camp leads. Ignoring it and thinking it's someone else's problem can damage our infrastructure.

#### Music
Music curation at NODE is intentional. We're not here to blast music into the void or contribute to the endless sea of sameness on the playa. We encourage selectors over performances, meaning thoughtful selection that fits the moment rather than loud or jarring music. From our hip hop BBQ (yes, real hip hop on the playa) to morning coffee sets that feel like sunrise, we match the vibe to the moment.

One important note: no overpowering volume. We're not a sound camp, and we want to stay on good terms with our neighbors. That means keeping levels in check and knowing when to turn it down. When there's no scheduled party, we operate with an open-decks policy. Play what you like, but keep it tasteful and energy-conscious. Music should enhance the moment, not overwhelm it.

#### Tent Structure & Quiet Hours
Quiet hours exist for a reason. If it's 4am and you're blasting dubstep from your Shiftpod, you're preventing others from getting their beauty sleep before the Purple Party. Don't be that Node.

#### Camp Cleanup
You may not be on MOOP patrol duty, but the trash you pass is your problem too. Take initiative. Communal effort never goes out of style. If trash is full, empty it and replace the bag. Collect empty cans. Pick up that broken zip tie. It takes a village.

### 🚗 Arrival & Exodus

#### Speed
The desert isn't a race track. Slow down for the dust, the critters, and the kids playing in goggles. The highway from Reno to BRC is a gauntlet of police, so don't tempt fate. The area around Burning Man is also ancestral lands. Respect the inhabitants, patience is a virtue.

#### In-Line Behavior
Be patient, be kind. The line is your first playa experience, so bring snacks, smiles, and tunes. It sets the tone. No drinking in your vehicle, wandering far from your car, smoking weed, or attempting to cut in line. Be respectful to the rangers and greeters; you'll be greeted at NODE with a hug and a smile soon enough.

#### Your Car at NODE
Only park where it's designated. Once the burn begins, vehicles should be invisible in form and footprint.

### 👮 Violations

At NODE, community and respect are foundational. If a camper violates our Code of Conduct, they will first receive a clear warning and guidance on how to get back into alignment with our values. After that warning, a two-strike policy applies: a first strike, then a second strike that results in serious consequences, such as restricted participation in future camp activities or, in some cases, removal from camp. This system exists to ensure fairness, accountability, and the safety and comfort of all NODE members.

## How Contribution Points Work

*NODE's contribution point system: how showing up and pitching in earns you priority for tickets, passes, and other limited perks.*

NODE uses a point system to help establish accountability and create a transparent, merit-based contribution structure. You earn points by participating in events and planning for the burn, in a multitude of ways: helping with build, camp strike, joining Secret Santa, coming on a Reno work trip, and more.

The system rewards the campers really giving their time and energy to NODE. When it comes to distributing perks such as NODE tickets, build week passes, tent rental availability, and other limited amenities, the point system leaderboard is consulted, and more active campers receive priority.

> **This framework underlies the camp's job/shift board.** The tasks and shifts you claim there are how you rack up points, so signing up is the most direct way to climb the leaderboard.

# 2. Onboarding, Prep & Packing

## Your Year at NODE

*A month-by-month guide from "I'm in!" to rolling onto the playa.*

Getting to Burning Man is a season-long build. Here's the rhythm of a NODE year so you know what's coming and when. The camper calls ramp up as the burn gets closer. They're the best way to meet your campmates before you're sharing a shade structure with them.

### Winter (January-March)

At this point the NODE leadership team is reaching out to suppliers and starting to prepare past and future campers for Burning Man.

- A monthly call to get to know your fellow campers.
- **In March, Burning Man tickets go on sale.** Don't miss the on-sale dates.

### Spring (April-May)

Suppliers start to get booked and paid, and the camper calls pick up to every other week.

- Bi-weekly calls for campers.
- **Dues are due end of May.** (See *Membership & Dues*.)

### Early Summer (June-July)

You should have your flights and transportation arranged by now.

- Book your flights and transportation to and from the playa.
- Start picking out outfits and finalizing fun gift ideas.
- Weekly calls begin.
- Last chance for tickets. The FOMO is real.

### August (Almost There)

- Sign up for camp jobs and activities.
- Get to know your fellow campmates on the weekly calls.
- Do a final pass on your *Packing Checklist* before you roll out.

## Camp Orientation & Welcome Tour

*What your first walk through camp covers, plus a checklist for whoever's giving the tour.*

When you arrive on playa, someone will take you on a welcome tour of camp. It's how you learn where everything is, how camp runs, and the handful of norms that keep 50-ish people living happily in the dust together. Read it before you arrive so nothing's a surprise, and use it as your script if you're the one giving the tour.

### First things first

- Drop your bags at the entrance to the tent shade structure so your hands are free for the walk-through.
- Start with a positive note. Thank them for being part of NODE, and stress the importance of the whole *and* the individual. Instill a sense of ownership for the camp.

### Private (Dining) Tent

This is our home on the playa, so treat it as such.

- **Job schedule:** Find the big printed job board here. Your shifts and duties live on it, and throughout the tour we highlight the areas of camp where various jobs get done.
- **Trash management:** Note where the trash bins, dumpster, food-waste mesh bag, and liquid-disposal buckets are, and learn the sorting so trash duty is easy for everyone.
- **Keep it tidy:** Minimize personal items strewn around the private tent. We clear off the dining table for every meal, and anything left on it gets mercilessly removed.

### Kitchen

- **Dishwashing:** Learn the three-bin dish station, the process, and the purpose of each bin.
- **Meal schedule:** Know when meals happen (see *Membership & Dues* for what's included).
- **Snack tent:** There's a grab-n'-go / leftovers prep area. Help yourself.
- **Hydration:** Water coolers and electrolyte (Liquid IV) packets are here. *Camp norm: don't post Liquid Death on socials. Officially, it doesn't exist.* <!-- editor: confirm whether the Liquid Death note is still relevant -->

### Bathrooms & Showers

- Give them a walk-through of the bathroom trailer and the protocol for using the toilets.
- **Half-step on the flusher before you go #2.** 💩
- Reiterate cleanliness.

### Bike Parking

- If you bought an e-bike through NODE, it'll be waiting in the storage container. If it needs wheels or pedals attached, follow the assembly tutorials in the bike emails.
- **Bikes should never be brought through the public tent into the private area of camp.**

### Public Stretch Tent & Bar

This is NODE's portal to the rest of the event.

- When NODEs hang out here, camp looks welcoming, bustling, and sexy. Spend time here.
- No personal camper items should be left in the public tent, ever. Leave things at your own risk.

### Fire Pit & Swings

Tertiary camp amenities. If you're into a fire, ask where the wood stash is kept.

### Tent Shade Structure

- **Tent placement:** Check with camp leads on exactly where to pitch your tent within the shade structure.
- **Power:** Tell whoever's setting you up your expected energy usage and any extra electronics you brought, so we can manage the camp's power load.

### For whoever's giving the tour

Keep it warm and useful. Hit these beats:

- [ ] Greet new campers with warmth and a smile
- [ ] Direct all cars to camp parking spots
- [ ] Offer a wagon and a hand unloading
- [ ] Help them find their rental tent in the containers (if they confirmed a rental ahead of time)
- [ ] Show them where to set up in tent city
- [ ] Answer questions and ease nerves
- [ ] Stress that personal items shouldn't be left strewn around camp
- [ ] Help set the tone for a great burn

### How we think about dues

We think about camp dues a lot. Please have a read.

Going to Burning Man is just like going on any other holiday or vacation: partying in Ibiza, skiing in Vail, sightseeing in Europe. Some people can afford to hitchhike and couch surf or stay in a youth hostel, others fly in private jets and swing five-star hotels. Both of these and everything in between is perfect!

Our mission is to maintain a **diverse family** who have different skills, different resources, and life experiences. We're interested in WHO you are, not WHAT you are. In keeping with that spirit we operate a **self-selecting sliding-scale** dues process: we take the budget, divide it among our roughly 50 members, and distribute it across a sliding scale (with one or two variables).

Why run this experiment? It's one thing to say we support diversity. We believe it's important to create, implement, and test a system that supports it and can be scaled. Everyone should have the option to remain anonymous to the greater group about their contribution, and no one should feel indebted to a single individual for paying for them. If you've been invited to camp at NODE, it's because a significant number of people want you here, and we believe that you, merely by being YOU, contribute to the mission, vision, and greater collective.

**Everyone pays something. Everyone participates.** Paying higher dues does not exclude you from participation (build, shifts, strike); paying lower dues does not come with the co-relative requirement of increased participation. We have allocated some partial scholarships for those who can't cover their dues, prioritizing returning members and then new members on a first-come, first-served basis. We're in it together, trusting each other to do what we can. A little bit of science + a little bit of art = a lot a bit of magic.

### What it costs

Putting on our camp and making this holiday happen is an expensive endeavor. The hot breakfasts and dinners, electricity, showers, toilets, storage, transportation, and admin all add up.

None of our camp members are paid for their participation at Burning Man. ## First-Timer Protips

*A few tips to make your first burn smoother.*

None of these are mandatory, but every one comes from NODEs who learned the hard way. Skim them before you pack.

### Seal a bag of clean clothes

When you're dirty and covered in dust, there is truly no respite like putting on a totally fresh, clean item of clothing. Some people only seal their final going-home outfit, so they're clean as they leave BRC. Others seal a few outfits throughout the week, which may just get you through some tough times at the burn. It takes a bit more planning and organization, but it pays dividends over the course of the trip. Just be sure to reuse the bags to minimize plastic waste.

### Take care of your feet (and vinegar baths)

Your feet are your #1 tool on the playa, and you should take extremely diligent care of them. Make sure your toenails are clipped before the trip, and pack functional closed shoes with lots of comfortable socks.

"Playa foot" is a malady unique to the Black Rock Desert caused by the alkali dust. It is, in essence, a chemical burn caused by exposing bare skin to the desert floor by not wearing socks and closed shoes. At NODE we believe the concern is a *little* overblown, and some breathing time is almost necessary to keep feet dry and healthy. We even have some campers who prefer sandals or spend time barefoot. HOWEVER, if you do let your feet touch the dust, it's critical you spend some extra time cleaning them off and treating them with a vinegar foot bath. The vinegar neutralizes the alkali, and you can add ice to help with soreness. More on playa foot: [Burning Man's playa foot guide](https://burningman.org/event/preparation/health-safety/playa-foot/).

### Glue your cuticles

Your hand and finger maintenance is also paramount at the burn. You'll be doing a lot of manual labor in a hot, dry environment, with fine dust getting in every crack and crevasse. Any cut or scratch you get will NOT heal until you return home where moisture exists, and hangnails will just tear and get worse. First thing when you arrive at NODE, take some super glue and apply a *small* amount to your cuticles to seal the gap between your skin and nails. It's a superhuman protective coating that doesn't come off with hand-washing or dry skin.

### Put a printed ID on your cup

This is just how it's done in BRC. To reduce plastic waste, every single individual at the burn carries a reusable cup at *all* times, and in a lot of cases that cup will be filled with alcohol, by a stranger! Enjoying a drink at another camp's bar is one of the simple joys of Burning Man, but you'll *always* need to present ID to show you're 21+. Burning Man even insists you should have your actual driver's license present, and a photocopy isn't admissible. But in 99% of cases, a printed copy of your license on your cup will do the job. Make sure you fully cover it with duct tape or it'll surely fade or tear.

### Rehearse your tent setup at home

There's nothing worse than getting to BM on Day 1, all excited to get settled, then fumbling with ropes and stakes for an hour in the heat. Especially if it's a new tent or you're unfamiliar with the setup, do a dry run at home. Toss it up in your backyard, brainstorm a layout for all your crap, and roll up to NODE knowing you'll be at home in no time.

### Put camp info on your phone's lock screen

A simple but genius failsafe in case you lose your phone on the playa. Set your lock screen to a photo of text showing your camp name, location, and maybe some other contact info. Most people in BRC are good samaritans, and if they find your lost phone with return instructions right there, they'll make it happen.

### Make your bike recognizable

Your bike is your trusty steed and the primary way around the event, but *everyone* will have one. Any time you arrive at an art piece in deep playa and disembark, your bike will be in a tangled mess of a thousand others, hard to relocate in the pitch black of night. Prepare a distinct decoration or two to spot yours easily, and ideally a bright light or a tail pole like a flag or LED stick.

**Lock buddy:** Lock your bike to a friend's rather than to a rack. That way two people remember the spot, and you've got double the custom decorations to home in on.

### Volunteer Roles

Sign up for shifts on the camp board. Every role below is a real job someone is counting on. Show up on time, and if you can't make your slot, find a swap.

#### Food & Kitchen

**Brunch Stocking & Prep.** Brunch is NODE's anchor meal, so this shift matters. Check the day's menu with the camp chefs or the kitchen binder, pull the needed ingredients from the kitchen container to the prep area, sanitize your hands, and help chop/mix/plate (aim for eggs + starch + protein + fruit). Clean as you go, then break down the dish station, wipe tables, turn off the propane, and make sure every fridge and door is shut tight.

**Dinner Cooking.** Your #1 job is helping the chefs: ask "who needs help?", chop/stir/serve, and keep food covered from dust. Check the kitchen binder for the night's meal and ingredients. Clean as you cook to minimize teardown, then run the dish station, wipe tables, shut off the propane, and confirm all fridges/doors are closed before you leave. **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm this year's dinner menu/schedule in the kitchen binder; the source schedule is year-specific.]**

**Buffet Setup (Chafing Dishes & Sternos).** Set up the warming line for brunch/dinner. Assemble each chafing rig (frame + water pan + food pans + lids), add 1-2 inches of water to the water pan (never let it touch the food pans), then light the Sternos *only after* the pans are in place, using the long lighter. Monitor water every 45-60 min and refill with hot water, keep lids closed between servings, and extinguish Sternos by sliding the lid over the flame. Safety: never light an empty pan (it scorches and warps), and keep Sternos away from table edges and anything flammable.

**Kitchen Cleanup.** Run the three-bin dish system in order: scrape scraps into the fishnet compost bag, then **Wash** (soapy water) → **Rinse** (clean water) → **Sanitize** (water + Steramine, 1-2 tablets per gallon, soak at least 1 minute) → air-dry or hand-dry. Refill/replace the bin solutions daily or whenever they get dirty. Never leave knives in the sink, wipe and sanitize prep surfaces, return tools to their homes, and MOOP-check the floor. Clean as you cook.

#### Bar & Coffee

**Coffee Duty.** We cold-brew with the Toddy system, prepped the night before to steep overnight. Clean the grounds bucket, add 1-2 bags of grounds, fill with water (not too full, it gets heavy), tie off the filter, and leave it steeping on the bar overnight (no need to refrigerate). Tips: if the spout clogs, lift the filter slightly; tilt the bucket at the end to get the last of it.

**Daytime Bartending.** Tend the NODE bar for your full shift: make drinks and keep it stocked with ice, mixers, and liquor. Serve from the large pre-mixed party batches; when they run out, switch to simple mixed drinks from the on-hand liquor. **Always ask for ID before serving alcohol.** Keep the bar tidy and do a quick MOOP sweep around your station.

**Evening Bartending.** Same core as daytime, at party volume. Prep drinks from the binder, scoop ice into a cooler/bowl, and mix heavy party batches in the 5/10-gal containers *at the bar* (they're too heavy to move full). **Always check ID: physical/hard IDs only, no photocopies, especially for anyone who looks under ~40, and be aware law enforcement runs stings.** During parties, a designated person checks IDs and stamps wrists at the ID station; send anyone without a stamp there. **Cooler Fill.** Keep the dining-area drink coolers stocked. Scoop ice from the ice machine with the designated clean vessel (never your hands), refill as needed, and empty melt-water into the greywater buckets.

### Emergency Protocols

Black Rock City is a harsh, unpredictable environment. Know these before you need them.

#### Dust Storms

Whiteouts roll in fast, can last hours, and bring low visibility, high winds, and hard breathing.
- **Your kit:** goggles with a tight seal (not just sunglasses), a snug facemask with good airflow (replaceable filters are ideal), a sealed personal water bottle, and lots of clean socks. Playa dust is highly alkaline, so change socks often and use vinegar foot baths to stay healthy.
- **When one rolls in:** do a fast lap and secure or tie down anything that could blow away (loose items are dangerous to us and neighboring camps). Cover or stow high-priority items: **CDJs & mixer, food, tent doors, air conditioners, storage-container doors, and distro/electrical boxes.**
- Use the camp's air compressor and leaf blower to clear dust off electronics and infrastructure.

#### Rain

Same prep as a dust storm, plus the complication of water and mud.
- The ground turns to mud almost instantly and mobility becomes nearly impossible. Expect any shoes/boots to be ruined.
- **Before it hits:** pick up anything that could soak or sink, cover/stow electronics (CDJs, Aggreko and other electrical gear), and pull couches and infrastructure inward from the stretch-tent edges.
- **Once it starts raining, SHELTER IN PLACE.** Walking on wet playa gouges craters and lengthens how long the ground takes to dry out.

#### Heat

- **Hydration is the single most important thing you do at BRC.** Always be drinking water or electrolytes and keep your cup on you. The camp always has a water station and electrolyte powder.
- **Cool down** with iced bandanas (a rag dipped in a cooler, worn on the neck), the air-conditioned tea tent (a sanctuary when you're struggling to cool off), and the green shade structure over tent village.
- **Reapply sunscreen constantly.** The peak-August sun plus zero humidity is brutal. Bring your own stash; travel sizes are great for on-the-go.
- Carry a **Camelbak** or hydration pack for time away from camp.

#### Law Enforcement (Trooper Visit)

Multiple agencies patrol BRC day and night, some undercover or in costume. Any illegal act they witness can lead to a citation or arrest. **Your best protection is to obey the law and know your rights.**
- **No public, visible illegal drug use in common areas.** Keep anything personal private and inside your own tent.
- **Cannabis is federally prohibited** on the playa (BRC is federal land). This is *not* a typical festival. Smoke smell carries and attracts Rangers, and glass paraphernalia (bongs, pipes) is prohibited too. Keep any personal apparatus hidden and used privately.
- **No nitrous / whippets at NODE.** They're loud, attention-grabbing, and a magnet for authorities.
- Know-your-rights references: [How to deal with cops at Burning Man (Reddit, 2022)](https://www.reddit.com/r/BurningMan/comments/w9rsrh/how_to_deal_with_cops_at_burning_man_2022_update/) and this [know-your-rights doc](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1lFAWDVzbRLkTM43Y588-ffFxuH7BfXzFbQ8ms7XF_xw/edit). **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm these third-party guides are still current.]**

## Lotus Belle Tent Setup

*Step-by-step instructions for raising a Lotus Belle tent at camp.*

Give yourself space and a second pair of hands. It goes much faster with two people. Work through the steps in order.

### What you need

- 1× peg hammer
- 12× smooth steel groundsheet pegs
- 20× super-strong threaded guy-line pegs
- 1× wooden centre pole (3 sections)
- 1× pole coaster (goes under the centre pole)
- 10× curved steel wall poles
- Wall canvas
- Roof canvas
- Green round groundsheet

### Setup steps

1. **Lay the groundsheet.** Spread it out and peg it down through the black elastic loops on the underside, using the smooth pegs (about 10 of them). *Tip: don't pull it too far out, or the wall canvas won't fit.*
2. **Open the wall poles.** Assemble all 10 wall poles into their full "C" shapes.
3. **Insert the wall poles.** Slide the poles into the wall canvas one at a time. Each pole goes into the black pockets, one at the top and one at the bottom. Pull and twist the pole until it fits between the velcro straps, then close the velcro to lock it in.
4. **Spread the wall on the groundsheet.** Lay the wall out flat on top of the groundsheet and hook the loops at its base onto the groundsheet pegs. *Tip: turning the pegs can make it easier to get the loops on.* **Make sure the door ends up in the right spot.**
5. **Place the guy-line pegs.** Set the guy-line pegs in a big ring around the tent. Use one section of the centre pole plus about 6" as your spacing guide. *Tip: check that each peg lines up straight from the centre.*
6. **Attach the wall guy lines.** Pull the wall guy lines out to the first ring of pegs. Put both guy lines (upper and lower) on the same peg.
7. **Hook up the groundsheet inside.** Go inside and hook the groundsheet onto the wall loops. You'll find them about 10" up from the floor, on the seam of the canvas wall panel.
8. **Attach the roof.** Drape the roof loosely onto the second row of pegs and pass the ends over them. Position the Lotus Belle logo so it sits above the doorway, and make sure the doorway arch is slightly higher there.
9. **Assemble the centre pole.** Fit the three sections together.
10. **Raise the centre pole.** Take it inside and stand it upright.
11. **Place the pole coaster.** Slide the pole coaster under the centre pole and check the pole is straight. **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm whether we actually have the pole coaster.]**
12. **Seal the roof to the walls.** While the roof is still loose, go around the inside doing up the velcro strip that joins the roof to the walls. *Important: this strip is not a vent; the roof is meant to lie flat against the wall. The strip creates a seal between the canvas sections.*
13. **Final adjustments.** Head back outside and tension the guy ropes until the canvas is smooth and flat.
14. **Outback & Deluxe models only.** Inside the tent, velcro the flap closed between the groundsheet and the walls.

### Finished

Now the fun part. Time to decorate.

### Recipes

## Music Guidelines

*How we think about sound at NODE: volume, vibe, and how to earn a DJ slot.*

Music is the answer. We've got a pristine Danley sound system ready for anyone itching to spin some records. Like every symphony, though, there are a few notes we need to harmonize on first.

### We're not a sound camp

First things first: we're not a sound camp. Respect the harmony of the city and the comfort of your campmates by keeping the volume reasonable. Basically, read the room. Play to the vibe and the time of day, and don't piss off our neighbors (too much).

### Play something different

High-energy tunes have their moment, but we're not trying to be the party hub of Burning Man. The goal is to be unique, dynamic, and always a touch above the predictable. The endless stream of deep house and deep tech echoing from every corner of the playa can stay where it is. We're here to offer a refreshing alternative. We aim to be the sonic chameleon of inner-city theme camps.

Different parts of the day call for different energy:

- **Mornings:** comforting melodies that pair with the aroma of rocket-fuel coffee.
- **Daytime parties:** a distinctive soundtrack that keeps the energy buzzing without overwhelming. House, techno, and disco are all fair game.
- **Quieter hours:** get weird. Offbeat stuff, jazzy whispers, futuristic electronica, soulful grooves, funky desert rhythms. A playa-worthy mix.

### Want a DJ slot?

Our programming structure leaves room for spontaneous magic while making sure everyone gets to play the music they love. If you've got a vision for a party, or want to secure a 1-2 hour slot for your curated set, we're all ears.

One small caveat: for vetting, you'll need to **submit a recorded mix or a playlist** of your planned music before you get a slot.

## Art Car & DMV Guide

*The reusable playbook for licensing and building a NODE mutant vehicle / golf cart: process, timeline, and the practical build constraints.*

Any vehicle that moves at Burning Man must be licensed as a Mutant Vehicle (MV) through Burning Man's **DMV (Department of Mutant Vehicles)**. Start early: the application window is short and closes months before the event.

### Licensing timeline

The MV application window shifts a bit each year, but it consistently opens in **mid-February** and closes in **mid-April**. For the 2025 cycle the exact dates were:

- **Applications open:** February 19, 2025, 12:00pm (noon) PST
- **Applications close:** April 16, 2025, 12:00pm (noon) PDT

A sample MV application (Google Drive) was saved as a reference for what the form asks for.

**[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm current-year MV application open/close dates on the official Burning Man DMV page]** Dates move annually; don't rely on the 2025 dates above.

### Build considerations

Design the vehicle around the cart's actual dimensions and stay lightweight. The constraints below all trace back to weight and the golf-cart chassis.

- **Weight vs. battery life.** The bigger we build, the more it weighs, which cuts into battery range. We also want as many people as possible to be able to ride, so keep the build light.
- **Height.** We can't go much taller: the cart is top-heavy on a short wheelbase, so added height hurts stability.
- **Sound.** There's no room or weight budget for a substantial sound system, but there is room for a generator.
- **Design is dimension-driven.** The build has to be based on the cart's dimensions, which keeps the design lightweight and does limit us to some extent.
- **Electrical / drivetrain.** Currently 8 cells; we should bump it to at least 12. We already have the charger; plan for new tires and new batteries.
