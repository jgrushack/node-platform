## Decor Sourcing Guide

*Where to buy NODE's public-tent decor, grouped by category. A reusable shopping reference distilled from the decor moodboard.*

Most items were scouted in pairs: a bulk/wholesale option on **Alibaba** and a faster, small-quantity option on **Amazon**. Use whichever fits your timeline and budget.

**[NEEDS UPDATE: verify vendor links]** the Amazon (`a.co`) short links, Alibaba product IDs, iPixelLEDs, and Temu share links below rot fast. Confirm each is live (and still the right product) before ordering.

### Sourcing principles

Keep these constraints in mind when choosing decor:

- **Cheap, high impact:** small price, big visual payoff.
- **MOOP-friendly and wind-friendly:** nothing flimsy that shreds or blows apart on playa.
- **Cohesive** with the public-tent furniture and existing NODE signage/aesthetic.
- **Unique:** we're aiming to not look like every other camp.
- **Main-tent direction:** start with a white canvas, flood it with colored LEDs, then add pops of color through color-blocking (solid colorful pillows, lanterns, etc.).

## Art Car & DMV Guide

*The reusable playbook for licensing and building a NODE mutant vehicle / golf cart: process, timeline, and the practical build constraints.*

Any vehicle that moves at Burning Man must be licensed as a Mutant Vehicle (MV) through Burning Man's **DMV (Department of Mutant Vehicles)**. Start early: the application window is short and closes months before the event.

### Licensing timeline

The MV application window shifts a bit each year, but it consistently opens in **mid-February** and closes in **mid-April**. For the 2025 cycle the exact dates were:

- **Applications open:** February 19, 2025, 12:00pm (noon) PST
- **Applications close:** April 16, 2025, 12:00pm (noon) PDT

A sample MV application (Google Drive) was saved as a reference for what the form asks for.

**[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm current-year MV application open/close dates on the official Burning Man DMV page]** Dates move annually; don't rely on the 2025 dates above.

### Build considerations

Design the vehicle around the cart's actual dimensions and stay lightweight. The constraints below all trace back to weight and the golf-cart chassis.

- **Weight vs. battery life.** The bigger we build, the more it weighs, which cuts into battery range. We also want as many people as possible to be able to ride, so keep the build light.
- **Height.** We can't go much taller: the cart is top-heavy on a short wheelbase, so added height hurts stability.
- **Sound.** There's no room or weight budget for a substantial sound system, but there is room for a generator.
- **Design is dimension-driven.** The build has to be based on the cart's dimensions, which keeps the design lightweight and does limit us to some extent.
- **Electrical / drivetrain.** Currently 8 cells; we should bump it to at least 12. We already have the charger; plan for new tires and new batteries.
