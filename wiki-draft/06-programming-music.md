## Signature Events & Programming

*The recurring events and parties that make NODE, NODE, plus a couple of our deep-playa traditions.*

Our week balances planned signature events with plenty of room for spontaneous magic. Think of this page as the evergreen idea bank of what we do year to year. The live, day-by-day schedule for the current burn lives in the **Calendar in your dashboard**, not here.

### Our signature events (the idea bank)

Not every one of these happens every year, but this is the menu we cook from:

- **Hip-Hop BBQ:** a daytime block party built around a grill and a hip-hop soundtrack. One of our biggest afternoons.
- **Themed dinners:** camp dinner is a nightly ritual, and the big ones get a theme. Past favorites include **Shabbat Dinner** and the all-white **Camp White Dinner**, plus rotating cuisines through the week (Mediterranean, taco night, Italian, build-a-bowl, stir fry, and an opening-night Pizza Party).
- **The Pickle Ball:** exactly what it sounds like: pickleball, playa-style, as an afternoon social.
- **Black Rock Philharmonic:** our camp's orchestra project. Members rehearse midweek and perform shows around the city (Center Camp, the Temple, the gates, and beyond).
- **Technologia:** our proper late-night music party.
- **Morning Coffee + Yoga:** most mornings open with rocket-fuel coffee and a yoga flow to ease into the day.
- **Welcome Ceremony & Pizza Party:** opening-night gathering to kick the week off together.
- **Color parties:** themed gatherings like the Purple Party where the dress code is the whole point.
- **Sunset art-car sets:** DJ sets on our art car to send off the sun.

### Roaming Street Market

*A mobile market that rolls through the city on Thursday night.*

Find us Thursday night between the Temple and the Man, about 200 ft to the right of the road, from 8PM until we get tired. Lots of other mobile (and mobile-ish) food and experience offerings join in too, so the goal is to make it as big as possible.

### Deep Playa Night Market

*A pop-up night market hidden out in deep playa.*

Look for us in deep playa from 10PM-12AM on Thursday night. Not sure you'll find us? Meet up at Namaskaram at the 6:00 portal at 9:00PM and ride out with us into deep playa around 9:30PM.

**Currency:** bring something to trade. At least 20 stickers or 5 patches for tradsies is recommended, though other currencies are welcome too.

## Music Guidelines

*How we think about sound at NODE: volume, vibe, and how to earn a DJ slot.*

Music is the answer. We've got a pristine Danley sound system ready for anyone itching to spin some records. Like every symphony, though, there are a few notes we need to harmonize on first.

### We're not a sound camp

First things first: we're not a sound camp. Respect the harmony of the city and the comfort of your campmates by keeping the volume reasonable. Basically, read the room. Play to the vibe and the time of day, and don't piss off our neighbors (too much).

### Play something different

High-energy tunes have their moment, but we're not trying to be the party hub of Burning Man. The goal is to be unique, dynamic, and always a touch above the predictable. The endless stream of deep house and deep tech echoing from every corner of the playa can stay where it is. We're here to offer a refreshing alternative. We aim to be the sonic chameleon of inner-city theme camps.

Different parts of the day call for different energy:

- **Mornings:** comforting melodies that pair with the aroma of rocket-fuel coffee.
- **Daytime parties:** a distinctive soundtrack that keeps the energy buzzing without overwhelming. House, techno, and disco are all fair game.
- **Quieter hours:** get weird. Offbeat stuff, jazzy whispers, futuristic electronica, soulful grooves, funky desert rhythms. A playa-worthy mix.

### Want a DJ slot?

Our programming structure leaves room for spontaneous magic while making sure everyone gets to play the music they love. If you've got a vision for a party, or want to secure a 1-2 hour slot for your curated set, we're all ears.

One small caveat: for vetting, you'll need to **submit a recorded mix or a playlist** of your planned music before you get a slot.
