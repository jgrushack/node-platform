## Our Values & Culture

*Who we are and what we stand for: NODE's mission, our value pillars, and how we live the Burning Man principles. Start here if you're new.*

### Our Mission

NODE builds aspirational playgrounds for future builders (you) in a forward-thinking (crazy awesome) way with a futuristic voice, helping you feel excited about the future and how bright it is.

### Our Value Pillars

Almost everything we make and do at NODE grows from six pillars. Each one carries a few sub-values we care about:

- **🎶 Music:** curation, performance, cohesion
- **🤸 Movement:** dancing, yoga, exercise
- **🫂 Community:** inclusivity, communication, collaboration
- **🦚 Expression:** creativity, art, fashion
- **🎭 Self:** nutrition, hydration, reflection
- **🧐 Curiosity:** imagination, initiative, humility

### Living the 10 Principles

The 10 Principles of Burning Man aren't just an idea but a guiding set of rules that help make Black Rock City possible. We come back to them all year: during every monthly camp call we discuss the principles and what they mean to us, and the NODE application displays the 10 principles and asks you to pick your favorite. Throughout the planning season, we keep hitting on different principles in our emails so everyone understands how important they are to Burning Man.

Two principles matter especially at NODE:

**Radical Inclusion.** Everyone deserves to feel welcome and included, regardless of background or beliefs. By creating an environment that values inclusivity, we build a strong sense of community and connection among our campers.

**Radical Self-Reliance.** NODE gives you a lot of support and resources, but we also encourage you to be self-sufficient and take responsibility for your own needs. Everyone should be prepared to fix their own broken bike, cook their own meals, and contribute to the community in meaningful ways. By promoting self-reliance, we empower you to take ownership of your Burning Man experience and develop important life skills along the way.

Two more principles take real effort:

**Participation.** Not everyone feels confident about how to get involved and make a meaningful contribution, and that's okay. Camp leads are here to provide clear guidance and encouragement so you can participate actively. If you're not sure how to plug in, just ask.

**Civic Responsibility.** We work to keep NODE a safe, respectful space, both at the festival and within camp. That means being mindful of each other's needs, leaving no trace, and following the event's rules so everyone feels comfortable and included.

### The 11th Principle: Consent

Beyond the official ten, NODE holds Consent as the 11th principle. Everyone is told there is a **zero-strike policy** at NODE: if anyone does not embody consent, they will be asked to leave immediately. There are no warnings. See the Code of Conduct for how consent and sexual safety are handled.

## The Node Foundation Charter

*The mission and purpose of The Node Foundation, our 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the legal and charitable backbone behind the camp.*

### Mission

To work towards a brighter future through community collaboration, art, music, and experience.

### Purpose

The Node Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the sum of its community members working towards a network of art, music, and experience, guiding discovery through events and charitable work. Our goal is to foster a supportive and inclusive environment that encourages creativity, collaboration, and personal growth, for our community and the world at large.

### Areas of Focus

1. **Community Building:** Organize and maintain a communal camp at Burning Man, providing infrastructure and resources to support the well-being and engagement of members. Facilitate regular meetings, workshops, and social events to build a sense of community and belonging among members and the greater public.
2. **Art and Creativity:** Provide resources, funding, and space for artists to develop and showcase their work, fostering a culture of creativity and innovation.
3. **Education and Skill Sharing:** Promote the exchange of knowledge and skills among members and the broader community.
4. **Sustainability and Environmental Responsibility:** Implement and promote sustainable practices such as waste reduction, recycling, and the use of renewable resources, leaving our world in a better place than we found it. Educate members on environmental stewardship and encourage eco-friendly practices both at events and in daily life.
5. **Inclusion and Diversity:** Create an inclusive and welcoming environment for individuals of all backgrounds, ensuring diversity is celebrated and respected while actively working to remove barriers to participation and foster a culture of openness and acceptance.
6. **Support and Outreach:** Engage in outreach activities to support and collaborate with local communities and nonprofit organizations.

## Code of Conduct

*The cornerstone agreement for every NODE member: how we treat each other, the camp, and the playa. Read it before you head to the desert.*

### 🚩 Vibe Check

Burning Man is mayhem. Although this code of conduct lays out a lot of the common-sense, golden-rule guidelines, there is no way it can encapsulate all of the unexpected edge cases, interpersonal conflict, or unquantifiable incidents. NODE leadership reserves the right to make judgement calls on whether an individual is crossing the line, clashing with the ethos of our camp, or disrupting the sanctuary of our desert home. For lack of a better term: a vibe check. If someone is demonstrating a severe lack of effort, inflicting their default-world emotional baggage on others, or attending BM for selfish reasons, a different camp may be a better option.

**TL;DR:** If your particular brand of humanity is hindering the smooth operation of our camp, you may be asked to step away from NODE to protect the positive experience of other campers.

### 🧘 Personal Space

At NODE, intimacy begins with respect. Always be mindful of others' physical and emotional space. Hugs are magic, but not mandatory. Read the room and the body language. No barging into other people's tents unannounced, or popping into a random RV to use the bathroom because all the stalls were taken.

### 🍆 Sexual Safety

Consent isn't just sexy, it's sacred. We uphold a strict **zero-strike policy**: any violation of consent will result in immediate removal from camp. Promiscuity and celebrating your body are part of what make Burning Man magical and empowering. Do not cross the line and take advantage of our communal trust. Nipple pasties? Let em' breathe ✅. Shirt-cocking ❌? Save it for the orgy dome. Common-sense stuff.

### 💰 Payment of Dues

Civic responsibility includes financial contribution. Your dues keep the cold brew flowing, the beats dropping, and the infrastructure standing. It's the bare minimum you can do to contribute to NODE, and issues with payment show an ignorance of the immense amount of capital it takes to pull off the event. If you're unsure about your dues, talk to a camp lead. Transparency is our jam. "Forgetting" to pay dues is a one-way ticket to open camping next year.

### 🫂 Inclusivity

Burning Man is where strangers become soulmates. Yes, plenty of us know each other from the default world. That doesn't mean those are the only people you ride out to the trash fence with. If you see someone on the fringe, pull them into the dance circle. We rise by lifting others.

### 🐴 Illicit Substances

Respect yourself, your community, and the law. NODE is not a place for reckless behavior, and we do not condone or approve of any illegal substances. Burning Man is on federal land, which makes marijuana illegal. Absolutely no smoking weed within view or smell of the public. Drug use should be treated with the utmost sensitivity. Make sure you know exactly what you're putting in your body. Do not discuss usage loudly, as that can beg for a visit from the police or feds.

If you're altering your consciousness, stay grounded in awareness and safety. No peer pressure, no shame, only care and consent. The Zendo is a great on-playa resource if you're not having a good time. Keep an eye on your friends, and speak up if you feel like something is off. NODE keeps narcan and fentanyl testing strips in our first aid kit.

### 🫡 Representing NODE On-Playa

Out in the wild of Black Rock City, you are NODE. Be kind, curious, and creative. Share your water, your wisdom, and your weirdness. We are the camp that gives more than we take.

### ☎️ Camp Calls

Show up. Engage. These aren't just logistics: they're how we build our dream before we build our city. Your voice matters. Calls are also how we get to know new members before heading into the desert.

### ❌ Strike

What goes up, must come down. Strike is a team effort and the camp's #1 priority starting Saturday morning. NODE strikes in the morning and afternoon before heading to the Man Burn and Temple. Abandoning strike duties to go explore the playa only puts a greater workload on the rest of the camp. When the whole camp works together to pack up, it gets done faster and allows for more time to enjoy BRC during the last few days of the event. Work hard, then play harder after.

### ⛺ Camp Life

#### Camp Resources & Amenities
It's your responsibility to familiarize yourself with the camp layout, storage containers, and how our camp operates. Knowing what's where prevents chaos and keeps our camp humming. Read the Notion or the NODE bible to brief yourself, and ask questions instead of for favors.

#### Camp Guests
Bring the vibes, but also bring the introductions. If your friends visit NODE, introduce them to the crew. Our hospitality runs deep, but trust is built with names and handshakes.

#### Social Media
Snap responsibly. Capture joy, not people's privacy. If you post, ask. Respect the magic and mystery of the playa. Some moments are meant to stay dusty. NODEs are here to be in the moment, not to sit in the public tent on Instagram. Refrain from posting publicly during Burn week.

#### Striking Your Tent, A/C, and Bike
Just because your burn ends doesn't mean your stuff vanishes. You are responsible for having your tent disassembled and out from under the large green shade structure at the time designated by camp leads. Bikes and A/Cs should be stored in NR1 when announced by camp leads, and failure to do so impedes our ability to strike the rest of camp. Strike with the same love and effort you used to set up.

### 🙏 Respect of Camp

#### Shower Length
Think spa vibes, not spa hours. Water is gold. Be mindful, be quick, and maybe bring a playlist under five minutes.

#### Tent Electricity
Don't treat the power strip like it's Coachella VIP. Share, rotate, and turn off when not in use. Use A/Cs to cool off, not to keep your tent an icebox 24/7.

#### Kitchen
Clean your dishes, close and secure the fridges, and stay out of the kitchen when people are cooking. Don't hoard ice. Keep the snack area clean. And remember: second helpings are cool, but only after everyone's had firsts.

#### Bikes
Park smart, don't block paths, use a kickstand, and use chargers with love and logic. Only enter and exit the camp through the designated areas. Being negligent with your bike can be dangerous to other campers, obstruct service vehicles, or damage your bike. When riding around BRC, please respect the strict 5 mph speed limit. Zipping across the playa puts a target on your back for rangers. It's about the journey, not the destination.

#### Bathrooms
Treat our portable porcelain thrones with reverence and respect. Wipe spills, tidy up, and keep your belongings out of communal spaces. Yes, they're co-ed. That means being considerate, civil, and conscious of others' privacy. Limit hand washing. It just wastes water, dries your skin out, and dust is the best soap anyways. Lubricate toilet bowls with water before doing your business. If you see an issue like a clogged toilet, leaky pipe, or overflowing tank, please tell one of the camp leads. Ignoring it and thinking it's someone else's problem can damage our infrastructure.

#### Music
Music curation at NODE is intentional. We're not here to blast music into the void or contribute to the endless sea of sameness on the playa. We encourage selectors over performances, meaning thoughtful selection that fits the moment rather than loud or jarring music. From our hip hop BBQ (yes, real hip hop on the playa) to morning coffee sets that feel like sunrise, we match the vibe to the moment.

One important note: no overpowering volume. We're not a sound camp, and we want to stay on good terms with our neighbors. That means keeping levels in check and knowing when to turn it down. When there's no scheduled party, we operate with an open-decks policy. Play what you like, but keep it tasteful and energy-conscious. Music should enhance the moment, not overwhelm it.

#### Tent Structure & Quiet Hours
Quiet hours exist for a reason. If it's 4am and you're blasting dubstep from your Shiftpod, you're preventing others from getting their beauty sleep before the Purple Party. Don't be that Node.

#### Camp Cleanup
You may not be on MOOP patrol duty, but the trash you pass is your problem too. Take initiative. Communal effort never goes out of style. If trash is full, empty it and replace the bag. Collect empty cans. Pick up that broken zip tie. It takes a village.

### 🚗 Arrival & Exodus

#### Speed
The desert isn't a race track. Slow down for the dust, the critters, and the kids playing in goggles. The highway from Reno to BRC is a gauntlet of police, so don't tempt fate. The area around Burning Man is also ancestral lands. Respect the inhabitants, patience is a virtue.

#### In-Line Behavior
Be patient, be kind. The line is your first playa experience, so bring snacks, smiles, and tunes. It sets the tone. No drinking in your vehicle, wandering far from your car, smoking weed, or attempting to cut in line. Be respectful to the rangers and greeters; you'll be greeted at NODE with a hug and a smile soon enough.

#### Your Car at NODE
Only park where it's designated. Once the burn begins, vehicles should be invisible in form and footprint.

### 👮 Violations

At NODE, community and respect are foundational. If a camper violates our Code of Conduct, they will first receive a clear warning and guidance on how to get back into alignment with our values. After that warning, a two-strike policy applies: a first strike, then a second strike that results in serious consequences, such as restricted participation in future camp activities or, in some cases, removal from camp. This system exists to ensure fairness, accountability, and the safety and comfort of all NODE members.

## How Contribution Points Work

*NODE's contribution point system: how showing up and pitching in earns you priority for tickets, passes, and other limited perks.*

NODE uses a point system to help establish accountability and create a transparent, merit-based contribution structure. You earn points by participating in events and planning for the burn, in a multitude of ways: helping with build, camp strike, joining Secret Santa, coming on a Reno work trip, and more.

The system rewards the campers really giving their time and energy to NODE. When it comes to distributing perks such as NODE tickets, build week passes, tent rental availability, and other limited amenities, the point system leaderboard is consulted, and more active campers receive priority.

> **This framework underlies the camp's job/shift board.** The tasks and shifts you claim there are how you rack up points, so signing up is the most direct way to climb the leaderboard.

### Ways to Earn Points

- Build Week
- Camp Strike
- Secret Santa
- Reno work trips
- Being a pod lead
