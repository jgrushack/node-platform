- **E Street:** 3:00, 4:30, 6:00, 7:30, 9:00
- **J Street:** 2:30, 3:45, 5:15, 7:15, 8:15, 9:30

## Fluff Patrol How-To

*How to roll out with a mist sprayer and gift cool relief to burners around BRC.*

Fluffing is the act of easing someone's hardship with a little relief or solidarity. In our case it means loading up a mist sprayer, cruising the playa, and spritzing overheated attendees with a cool mist to help them beat the heat and dust.

For the origin of the term, see the [Burning Man glossary](http://burningman.org/culture/glossary/) (a "fluffer" is a volunteer who supports other teams in the field, e.g. by bringing them water) and the essay ["Fluffing in the Workplace."](https://medium.com/@lilie.fine/fluffing-in-the-workplace-81565b2f5188)

### What to bring on a run

- An **electric bike**, since you'll cover a lot of ground.
- A **mist sprayer**, chilled and filled completely.
- A **secure way to mount the sprayer** to your bike for travel.
- **Ample sun and dust protection** for yourself.
- Optional: the **mobile speaker** to set the mood.

### How to fluff

Be friendly and approachable. This is a fantastic excuse to meet people, give a gift, and explore the city. Roll up, offer a mist, and enjoy the reactions.

When your sprayer runs low, you have two options: swing back to NODE for a refill, or venture into other camps and charmingly beg for water (and ice, if they're feeling generous). A fun trade: offer a camp-wide **mist-stravaganza** in exchange for a refill.

### Add-ons

A few drops of essential oil make the mist extra restorative:

- Eucalyptus
- Lavender

### Kit checklist

- Sprayers
- Ice
- Mobile speaker

## Lotus Belle Tent Setup

*Step-by-step instructions for raising a Lotus Belle tent at camp.*

Give yourself space and a second pair of hands. It goes much faster with two people. Work through the steps in order.

### What you need

- 1× peg hammer
- 12× smooth steel groundsheet pegs
- 20× super-strong threaded guy-line pegs
- 1× wooden centre pole (3 sections)
- 1× pole coaster (goes under the centre pole)
- 10× curved steel wall poles
- Wall canvas
- Roof canvas
- Green round groundsheet

### Setup steps

1. **Lay the groundsheet.** Spread it out and peg it down through the black elastic loops on the underside, using the smooth pegs (about 10 of them). *Tip: don't pull it too far out, or the wall canvas won't fit.*
2. **Open the wall poles.** Assemble all 10 wall poles into their full "C" shapes.
3. **Insert the wall poles.** Slide the poles into the wall canvas one at a time. Each pole goes into the black pockets, one at the top and one at the bottom. Pull and twist the pole until it fits between the velcro straps, then close the velcro to lock it in.
4. **Spread the wall on the groundsheet.** Lay the wall out flat on top of the groundsheet and hook the loops at its base onto the groundsheet pegs. *Tip: turning the pegs can make it easier to get the loops on.* **Make sure the door ends up in the right spot.**
5. **Place the guy-line pegs.** Set the guy-line pegs in a big ring around the tent. Use one section of the centre pole plus about 6" as your spacing guide. *Tip: check that each peg lines up straight from the centre.*
6. **Attach the wall guy lines.** Pull the wall guy lines out to the first ring of pegs. Put both guy lines (upper and lower) on the same peg.
7. **Hook up the groundsheet inside.** Go inside and hook the groundsheet onto the wall loops. You'll find them about 10" up from the floor, on the seam of the canvas wall panel.
8. **Attach the roof.** Drape the roof loosely onto the second row of pegs and pass the ends over them. Position the Lotus Belle logo so it sits above the doorway, and make sure the doorway arch is slightly higher there.
9. **Assemble the centre pole.** Fit the three sections together.
10. **Raise the centre pole.** Take it inside and stand it upright.
11. **Place the pole coaster.** Slide the pole coaster under the centre pole and check the pole is straight. **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm whether we actually have the pole coaster.]**
12. **Seal the roof to the walls.** While the roof is still loose, go around the inside doing up the velcro strip that joins the roof to the walls. *Important: this strip is not a vent; the roof is meant to lie flat against the wall. The strip creates a seal between the canvas sections.*
13. **Final adjustments.** Head back outside and tension the guy ropes until the canvas is smooth and flat.
14. **Outback & Deluxe models only.** Inside the tent, velcro the flap closed between the groundsheet and the walls.

### Finished

Now the fun part. Time to decorate.

## Morning Coffee Bar SOP

*Our repeatable menu, recipes, and setup for serving morning coffee at camp.*

This is our standard operating procedure for the morning coffee bar. The menu and recipes below are the repeatable part, so reuse them each year. **Note: this SOP was last revised in 2023.** The original detailed buy-list (with specific product links) has been dropped as stale; refresh quantities and sources against current needs before shopping.

### Menu

- Iced coffee
- Iced latte
- Mocha
- **Flavored lattes:** vanilla, caramel, hazelnut, matcha, chai, lavender, turmeric
- **Espresso martini** (boozy)
- **Irish coffee** (boozy)

### Recipes

**Cold brew base.** We cold-brew in bulk: coffee grounds in a 10-gallon bucket fitted with a large mesh filter, steeped overnight, then pulled for the day's iced drinks. This base carries the iced coffee, iced lattes, mochas, and flavored lattes.

**Flavored lattes.** Build on the cold-brew base with milk (see stock list) plus the flavor: a pump of flavored syrup (vanilla, caramel, hazelnut, lavender), matcha, chai concentrate, or turmeric. Finish with cinnamon or chocolate powder as appropriate.

**Espresso martini.** Shake vodka, espresso, Kahlúa, and simple syrup with plenty of ice; strain into a glass and garnish with a few coffee beans. (Quantities to taste; dial in a house ratio on the first morning.)

**Irish coffee.** Stir brown sugar into hot coffee until dissolved, add whiskey, and top with whipped cream.

### Stock list (categories)

Keep these on hand; buy quantities to camp size:

- **Milks:** shelf-stable almond, oat, and non-dairy creamer.
- **Sweeteners:** brown sugar, brown agave nectar, simple syrup.
- **Flavored syrups:** vanilla, caramel, hazelnut, chocolate, lavender.
- **Powders & specialty:** matcha, cinnamon powder, chocolate powder, turmeric powder, chai concentrate.
- **Toppings & garnish:** whipped cream, vanilla ice cream (for affogato-style), coffee beans, cinnamon sticks, gingersnaps.
- **Bar supplies for cocktails:** vodka, whiskey, Kahlúa, espresso concentrate, simple syrup, cocktail shaker.
- **Gear:** milk frother, shaker, 10-gallon buckets, large mesh coffee filter.

## Post-Burn Photo Sharing

*How to upload your playa photos to the communal NODE Drive, plus the etiquette that keeps it safe.*

We keep a big communal NODE Google Drive with photos going back several years, a beautiful collection of memories made on-playa. It's also useful: we mine it for social media, to remember infrastructure and camp layout, and to plan menus and order equipment. Please contribute your shots.

### How to upload

Most of us have our photos on our phones, so uploading straight from mobile is easiest.

1. **Install the Google Drive app.** [iOS](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/google-drive/id507874739) · [Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.docs)
2. **Open the shared photo folder.** **[NEEDS UPDATE: current shared Drive link.]** *(Because the Drive is publicly editable, the folder link is kept here behind member login rather than posted publicly.)*
3. **Create your own new folder** inside it (named for you) and drop all your photos there.

### Etiquette (please read)

- The Drive is **public and editable by anyone with the link.** Be careful: **do not delete anyone else's folders or previous years' collections.**
- **No photos of illicit activities or nudity.**
- Keep the link within the camp (it lives behind member login for this reason).

### Upload tips

The Google Drive app can be finicky on large batches. You may need to keep the app open and in the foreground for the upload to keep processing. Some people turn off auto-lock so the screen doesn't sleep mid-upload ([how to keep an iPhone display on](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/keep-the-iphone-display-on-longer-iph7117338a8/ios)). Thanks for jumping through the hoops. The archive is worth it.
