## Build & Strike Playbook

*Your field manual for standing NODE up and taking it down: the ordered build sequence, per-structure hardware counts, power distribution, teardown order, and off-season container upkeep.*

This is the evergreen how-to distilled from past build and strike cycles. Treat it as the default plan and adjust to the current year's layout, crew size, and container contents. Anything tagged **[NEEDS UPDATE]** is a known refresh item. Confirm it before you rely on it.

---

### Build Order

The on-playa build runs roughly Wednesday through Saturday before the event opens, preceded by a Reno pre-build work session. Sequence flows: **utilities → containers → core structures → plumbing → secondary structures → decor & lighting.** Do the load-bearing infrastructure first; save decor for last.

#### Pre-Build: Reno (Sun-Tue)
- Food: pickup runs (Costco/Metrorack, US Foods, Bonanza) and prep (sous vide, protein prep); pack the kitchen trailer.
- Pick up liquor for gifting to placement/BLM staff.
- DeMOOP (clear leftover matter-out-of-place from storage) and a Home Depot run.
- Shopping: 2x4s for couch backs (~25), foldable table, measuring tape + survey flags, power inverter.
- Confirm survey equipment: measuring tape, flags.
- Pack trucks; secure the Danley subs and other speakers for transport.

#### Day 1: Wednesday (Arrival, Survey, Utilities, Core Structures)
1. **Depart early**, final gas stop near Gerlach, drive to the plot.
2. Park, set up Starlink, wait for placement to arrive and confirm your footprint.
3. **Survey:** flag the four corners of each storage container; call the container hauler to deliver and land containers, then open them.
4. **Utilities:** water resupply run, call electric, pick up from United (site services).
5. **Empty containers:** clear the wood area and the laundry/pillow area; pull bulk aisle items (see Structure Build Details → Container unload).
6. Lay **ground tarps**.
7. Set up **swamp coolers**.
8. Raise the **kitchen tent**.
9. **Run electric** to the kitchen.
10. Raise the **green shade structure**; park cars on the back line as wind/anchor support.
11. Hang **aluminet** shade over kitchen and bathroom.
12. Set up **work lighting**.
13. Pull and pitch the build team's shiftpods.

#### Day 2: Thursday (Public Tent, Plumbing, Shade, Walls)
- **Public tent:** raise tent, then install bar, couches, DJ booth, carpets. **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm carpet/tent footprint dimensions; prior years used 65x49 tent area and 14x48 carpet runs.]**
- **Plumbing:** kitchen sink, greywater, bathroom (shower pumps, A/C install).
- Snack tent.
- Dining shade (12x20 brown shades). **[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm dining shade size vs. current layout.]**
- Walls.
- Bike racks and bike charging station.
- Repair the meat flap (shade entrance flap).
- Clean bathroom.
- Post the build schedule sign.

#### Day 3: Friday (Secondary Structures, Decor, Lighting)
- Bell tent (Lotus Belle) + its aluminet shade.
- Swings.
- Decor: floofs, ceiling drapes, curtain behind DJ booth, Nanoleafs.
- Lighting: Tent City.
- Couch back supports.
- DJ equipment.
- Sign repair.
- Public tent entranceway.
- Bathroom shelf.

#### Day 4: Saturday (Finish Out)
- Job board and Polaroid board.
- Mushroom lamps.
- Perimeter lighting.
- Projectors.
- NODE storefront schedule sign.
- Workshop clean-up.

### Power & Generator Distribution

Power flows from the generator to distribution points at the containers. Run electric to the kitchen early on Day 1, right after the kitchen tent goes up. Baseline distro assignments:

- **NR1 distro:** none.
- **NR2 distro:** charge drills; NR2 interior lights.
- **NR3 distro:** plug in fridges.
- **NR4 distro:** pumps, lights, radio.
- **Tent City / build camp:** power grid runs off the green shade structure area for build-team shiftpods and A/Cs.
- **RV hookups:** confirm amperage per rig (commonly 30amp) before build.

Work lighting and drill chargers run off the workshop (NR2) supply during build.

### Strike / Teardown Sequence

Teardown is paced against the two burn nights. Work backward: soft decor and public-facing structures come down first (while the party winds down), infrastructure and utilities last.

**[NEEDS UPDATE: confirm the Man Burn and Temple Burn nights/times against the official Burning Man calendar each year; they anchor this whole schedule. Baseline below assumes Man Burn Saturday ~9pm, Temple Burn Sunday ~8pm.]**

#### Saturday (before Man Burn)
- Public tent: DJ booth, carpets, couches, bar.
- Perimeter lighting.
- Decor: floofs, mushroom lights, ceiling drapes, Nanoleafs.
- Tea tent.
- Swings.
- Bike racks.
- *Man Burn ~9pm.*

#### Sunday (before Temple Burn)
- Walls.
- Sign.
- Consolidate food.
- Tables / chairs.
- Downgrade to a skeleton kitchen: leave 1 grill + 1 induction plate running.
- Take down all tent lighting.
- Bag pillows.
- Snack / dish tent.
- *Temple Burn ~8pm.*

#### Monday (departure day)
- **Wake up at 6am.**
- Shiftpods.
- A/Cs.
- Green shade structure (and Tent City power grid).
- Kitchen tent: plumbing, then power.
- Dining tent.
- Floor tarps.
- Bikes.
- **Target: depart by 11am.**

### Off-Season Storage Maintenance

Run this during the May Reno work trip, before Burning Man season. Goal: containers packed, clean, labeled, and repaired so build week starts fast.

#### Whole-site / passive tasks
- Inspect and, if needed, replace water lines (freeze damage over winter).
- General emptying and cleaning of all containers.
- Get rid of wood you aren't using.
- Have tools ready and staged for build.
- Confirm a trailer is available to bring in food.

#### Build-ahead projects (do in Reno, not on playa)
- Build the new bar (wood); assign a build lead. Design it to build easily and pack flat; keep the removable front panels for the Nanoleafs.
- Build the DJ booth (wood).
- Build NR5 shelving.
- Acquire dining tables and additional public-tent couch wood.
- Source more carpets for the larger public area.
- Wood needs: public bar plywood (see Bar), DJ booth, NR5 shelving. Steel needs: roof decks.

#### Per-container checklist

- Fix the broken shelf.

**NR2: Workshop**
- Remove one shelf and set up the toolbench.

- Clear out dormant members' bins.

**NR3: Kitchen**

- Appraise cleanliness of dishes and pots (double-check even if they look clean); clean any dirty or exploded items.

- Label all bins correctly.

### Container Inventory

NODE runs five shipping containers, **NR1-NR5**, each with a fixed primary role. Keep one canonical inventory record per container and re-verify it every May; contents drift year to year. **[NEEDS UPDATE: re-verify all container contents against actual state during the annual May work trip.]**

**Canonical role + baseline contents**

| Container | Role | Typical contents |
|---|---|---|
| **NR1** | Bikes & A/Cs | Bikes, A/C units, pole cart, throne, BBQ, back benches, folding tables, bar chairs, large stretch-tent parts |
| **NR2** | Workshop | Toolbench, drills/bits, bar wood + bar hardware (dedicated tupperware), a stretch tent, speakers, 1x swamp cooler |
| **NR3** | Kitchen | Metal prep tables, white fridge, lowboy, fridges, 1x swamp cooler, kitchen equipment |
| **NR4** | Bathroom | Plumbing, shower pumps, water heater, pillows, couch cushions, linens |
| **NR5** | Storage | Green shade structure, NODE sign, mattresses, ground tarps, ketacouches, shiftpods, shelving/aluminet, plywood, dining tables, swamp coolers, personal bins |

**Inventory format (reusable template).** For each container, record:
- **Container ID + role** (NR#, e.g., "NR3: Kitchen").
- **Contents list**, grouped by aisle/zone, so you know what to unload first on build day.
- **Reference photos** of the packed container (the "NODE bible"), re-shoot each May.
- **Bin labels:** every stored bin tagged with owner, year last used, weight flag (e.g., "heavy"), and contents. Print and laminate labels during the Reno trip.

**Bin-shelf slot system:** any camper storing a personal bin gets one shelf slot. Their belongings must fit within that slot, so nobody has to move anyone else's bin to get theirs out. Annually: clear stale/abandoned bins after notifying owners (unclaimed bins from members who haven't attended in several years get discarded).
